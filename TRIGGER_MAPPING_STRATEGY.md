## TASK 2: MARKETING AWARENESS STAGE

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Awareness Stage** | **SOLUTION-AWARE** |
| **Why This Stage Dominates** | They already know AI is the problem (headlines did that work). They already know "building with AI" is the direction (LinkedIn/Twitter content did that). What they lack is the SPECIFIC vehicle -- which workshop, which roadmap, which 120 minutes will actually get them from frozen to shipping. They don't need to be convinced AI matters. They need to be convinced THIS is the path. |
| **What Must Be Avoided** | Do NOT explain why AI is important. Do NOT educate on "what is agentic AI." Do NOT sell the CATEGORY (AI upskilling). Sell the MECHANISM (live build, ship in 120 min, specific tools). Any ad that starts with "AI is changing the world..." will be scrolled past instantly. They've heard it 500 times. |

## TASK 3: EMOTION --> ANGLE --> STRATEGY MAP

| Core Emotion | Strategic Angle | Why This Works Now |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Existential career fear** | The identity split: consumer vs builder | 49% of Indian devs already feel this fear. The language "consumer vs builder" gives them a frame to self-identify and a clear exit. It names the problem without insulting them. |
| **Shame of falling behind** | The junior-senior inversion | Companies are mandating Cursor/Copilot. Juniors adapt faster. Seniors feel exposed. This is a private wound that no one talks about publicly. Naming it builds instant trust. |
| **Frustration with stalled progress** | The tutorial hell exit | 8+ months of content consumption with zero output is the dominant pattern. Promising a SHIPPED outcome in 120 minutes directly counters the pattern of endless learning. |
| **Suspicion toward free content** | The anti-webinar webinar | Past burns from pitch-disguised-as-workshops created deep skepticism. Addressing it head-on ("This isn't slides. This is live code.") flips the objection into a trust signal. |
| **Hope for transformation** | The one-ship breakthrough | Their deepest desire is shipping ONE real thing. Not a course completion. Not a certificate. One deployed product. Framing the workshop around a single tangible output matches their readiness to act. |
| **FOMO / social comparison** | The builder migration | 5,000+ registered. 167K GitHub stars on OpenClaw in a week. The builders are already moving. This isn't aspiration -- it's a migration happening now. Those who stay behind will notice. |
| **Financial anxiety** | The salary gap reality | 56% salary premium for AI-skilled devs is a market fact, not a promise. It converts the abstract "learn AI" into concrete rupee-value of inaction. Every month of delay has a measurable cost. |
| **Desire for respect** | The "AI person" identity claim | They want to be the one their manager names. They want to answer AI questions, not ask them. The workshop is positioned as the entry ticket to that identity -- not through knowledge, but through having built something. |

## TASK 4: STRATEGY SUMMARY

### Emotions to prioritise (in order):

1. Fear of obsolescence -- opens attention
2. Shame of the consumer-builder gap -- creates personal urgency
3. Hope of a specific, timed transformation -- provides the exit
4. FOMO from peer movement -- adds social pressure to act now

### Mindsets that must be challenged or reframed:

- "Learning = progress" must become "shipping = progress"
- "I need more time to prepare" must become "preparation is the trap"
- "Free webinars are pitches" must become "this one builds live, you decide"
- "I'm too senior for a workshop" must become "AI doesn't care about your YOE"
- "I need to learn Python first" must become "the new language is English"

### What should NOT be said in ads:

- Do not explain what AI is
- Do not explain what agentic AI means
- Do not use the word "learn" as the primary verb
- Do not promise vague transformation ("change your life")
- Do not lead with the trainer's bio or credentials
- Do not use the phrase "AI is the future"
- Do not say "beginner-friendly" (triggers identity resistance in seniors)
- Do not use "masterclass" (overused, triggers cynicism)

### What the audience must feel BEFORE copywriting starts:

- "This person knows exactly what I'm going through"
- "This isn't theory, something will be BUILT"
- "People like me are already doing this"
- "The cost of not showing up is higher than 2 hours"
- "This might be the one that actually works"
